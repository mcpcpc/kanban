Help & Documentation

Quick Start

  4. Print Cards — Generate cards to place in bins
  5. Start Scanning — Use Quick Scan when bins need restocking
Key Concepts
Part
An item being tracked in inventory, identified by part number and manufacturer.
Bin
A physical location that holds inventory. A bin can contain multiple kanbans for different parts.
Kanban
A visual signal linking a part to a bin with replenishment rules (reorder point, quantity, max).
Signal
Triggered when a kanban quantity is depleted — indicates replenishment is needed.
Reorder Point
The stock level at which a signal should be triggered. Best practice: (Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock.
Safety Stock
Buffer inventory to protect against demand variability and supply delays.
Lead Time
Days required to receive new stock after ordering.
Workflows

Daily Operations

  1. Worker depletes a kanban → takes kanban card
  2. Scan card → records Signal
  3. Warehouse sees active signals on Dashboard
  4. Starts replenishment → Restocking
  5. Receives material → Restocked
  6. Returns card to bin with restocked material

Inventory Monitoring

  1. View Inventory page for stock levels
  2. Check days of supply vs. lead time
  3. Filter by status to find problems
  4. Export CSV for purchasing or ERP

Cycle Counting

  1. Go to Inventory → select a part
  2. Click "Adjust" button
  3. Count physical stock in bin
  4. Enter new quantity with reason
  5. System logs adjustment for audit
Scanning Guide

Use the Quick Scan page to record kanban events. Select the appropriate action before scanning:

Action When to Use Quantity
Signal Kanban quantity depleted — pulling this card to request replenishment Not needed
Restocking You've started gathering material to refill this kanban (optional intermediate step) Not needed
Restocked Kanban has been refilled — returning the card with replenished material Enter quantity received

Tip: Most users only need Signal (when pulling a card) and Restocked (when returning a card with material).

Frequently Asked Questions
What happens when I set a kanban to inactive?
Inactive kanbans are excluded from all forward-looking inventory calculations:
  • Reorder Point: The kanban's reorder point is removed from the part's total reorder threshold
  • Kanban Quantities: Not included in total kanban quantity calculations
  • Scan Events: Scanning an inactive kanban card will show a warning and won't record events
  • Inventory Adjustments: Adjustment events are only logged to active kanbans

Note: Historical data (past events, demand history) is preserved even after deactivating a kanban.

How is "Days of Supply" calculated?
Days of Supply = Quantity on Hand ÷ Average Daily Demand. The average daily demand is calculated from restock events over the last 30 days. If there's no restock history, days of supply cannot be calculated.
What's the difference between "Warning" and "Low Stock" status?
  • Low Stock: Quantity on hand is at or below the sum of all active kanban reorder points for that part
  • Warning: Days of supply is less than or equal to the part's lead time — you may run out before new stock arrives
How does lead time affect inventory status?
Lead time (set on each part) is compared against days of supply. If your days of supply is less than your lead time, the system shows a "Warning" status — indicating you should reorder now to avoid a stockout before new material arrives.
Can I have multiple kanbans for the same part?
Yes. You might have the same part stocked in multiple bins across different areas. Each kanban has its own reorder point and quantity. The inventory view aggregates all active kanbans when calculating totals for a part.
How should I set my reorder point?
The recommended formula is: Reorder Point = (Estimated Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock. Enter your estimated daily demand on the kanban form, and the system will suggest a reorder point. You can compare this against actual demand shown on the Inventory page.
What is safety stock and how do I determine it?
Safety stock is buffer inventory that protects against unexpected demand spikes or supply delays. A simple approach: set it to cover 1-2 days of estimated demand. For critical parts, consider: (Max Daily Demand × Max Lead Time) - (Avg Daily Demand × Avg Lead Time).
What is estimated daily demand?
The expected units consumed per day for this kanban. Use this to calculate reorder points for new parts without history, or to plan for expected demand changes. Compare against actual demand on the Inventory page and adjust as needed.
